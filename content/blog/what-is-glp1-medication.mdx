## The Weight Loss Medication Changing Everything

Over the past few years, you've probably heard the buzz around Ozempic, Wegovy, and Mounjaro. These medications — all classified as **GLP-1 receptor agonists** — have become the most talked-about advance in obesity medicine in decades. But what exactly are they, and how do they work?

This article breaks down the science in plain language, so you can make an informed decision about whether GLP-1 therapy might be right for you.

---

## What Is GLP-1?

GLP-1 stands for **glucagon-like peptide-1** — a hormone your body naturally produces in your gut after you eat. Its job is to:

- Signal to your pancreas to release insulin (which lowers blood sugar)
- Suppress glucagon (a hormone that raises blood sugar)
- Slow the movement of food through your stomach
- **Communicate with your brain's appetite center** to reduce hunger

That last point is crucial. GLP-1 tells your brain you're full — even when you haven't eaten as much as usual. This is why people on GLP-1 medications often describe feeling "full faster" and having significantly reduced food cravings.

---

## How Do GLP-1 Medications Work?

GLP-1 receptor agonists **mimic** the natural GLP-1 hormone, but they're engineered to last much longer in your body. Natural GLP-1 is broken down within minutes — these medications last days or even a week.

The result:

- **Reduced appetite** — You feel satisfied with smaller meals
- **Slower gastric emptying** — Food stays in your stomach longer, reducing the urge to eat again quickly
- **Blood sugar regulation** — Stabilized blood sugar reduces energy crashes and cravings
- **Direct action on brain receptors** — Signals to reward centers in the brain reduce "food noise" and emotional eating

## How Much Weight Can You Lose?

Clinical trials have shown impressive results:

| Medication | Active Ingredient | Avg. Weight Loss |
|------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Wegovy | Semaglutide 2.4mg | 15–17% body weight |
| Ozempic | Semaglutide 0.5–2mg | 10–14% body weight |
| Mounjaro | Tirzepatide | 20–22% body weight |
| Zepbound | Tirzepatide | 20–22% body weight |

For a 220 lb person, a 15% loss equals **33 lbs** — results that historically required bariatric surgery.

## Who Qualifies for GLP-1 Therapy?

GLP-1 medications are typically prescribed for adults who meet **one of the following**:

- **BMI ≥ 30** (obesity)
- **BMI ≥ 27** with at least one weight-related health condition (Type 2 diabetes, high blood pressure, sleep apnea, etc.)

They are **not** appropriate for people with:
- Personal or family history of thyroid cancer (medullary type)
- Multiple Endocrine Neoplasia syndrome type 2 (MEN 2)
- History of pancreatitis
- Type 1 diabetes

## What Should You Expect?

### Weeks 1–4
Most patients start on a low dose. Common early side effects include mild nausea, which typically subsides as your body adjusts.

### Months 1–3
Appetite noticeably decreases. Most patients report eating significantly less without feeling deprived. Weight loss begins — typically 1–2 lbs per week.

### Months 3–6
Dosage may be adjusted upward. Weight loss continues, often accelerating. Energy levels improve.

### Months 6–12
Most patients reach 10–20% weight loss. Blood pressure, blood sugar, and cholesterol markers typically improve alongside weight loss.
