## Step 1: Complete Your Online Health Assessment (~2 minutes)

The first step is a short questionnaire designed to determine if you're a candidate for GLP-1 therapy. You'll answer questions about:

- Your weight loss goals
- Your current height and weight
- Basic health history (certain conditions can disqualify candidates for safety reasons)
- Previous weight loss attempts

This isn't just a marketing funnel — it's a **genuine medical screening**. GLP-1 medications are not appropriate for everyone, and responsible platforms filter out patients who may be at risk.

**What gets you disqualified?**
- History of pancreatitis
- Personal or family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma
- Multiple Endocrine Neoplasia syndrome type 2 (MEN 2)
- Type 1 diabetes
- Active eating disorder

If you don't meet any of these exclusion criteria and have a qualifying BMI, you'll move forward.

## Step 3: Complete Your Medical Intake Form

After payment, you'll complete a detailed medical intake — this is the form your prescribing physician actually reviews. It covers:

- Personal information and shipping address
- Current medications
- Known drug allergies
- Detailed medical history relevant to GLP-1 safety
- Self-reported vitals (optional but helpful)
- Previous GLP-1 use, if any
- Digital consent and signature

**This step is critical.** The more detailed and accurate your intake, the faster your provider can review and approve your case.

> Pro tip: Have a list of your current medications handy before you start the intake form. It takes 5–10 minutes to complete.

---

## Step 4: Provider Review (24–48 Hours)

Once your intake is submitted, a licensed physician reviews your case. They check:

- That you meet clinical criteria for GLP-1 therapy
- That there are no contraindications based on your medical history
- The appropriate medication and starting dose for your profile

If your case is straightforward, approval typically happens within 24 hours. In some cases, the provider may reach out with follow-up questions before prescribing.

## What Happens Next?

Starting medication is just the beginning. Here's what to expect in your first weeks:

### Week 1–2: Low starting dose
Most programs start with a very low dose to minimize side effects. Common early side effects (mild nausea, reduced appetite) typically resolve within 2–4 weeks.

### Week 4+: Dose escalation
Your provider will gradually increase your dose based on how you're tolerating the medication and your weight loss progress.

### Monthly: Check-ins
Depending on your plan, you'll have scheduled check-ins with your provider via secure messaging or video call. Use these to report side effects, ask questions, and discuss any adjustments.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I get GLP-1 medication if I don't have diabetes?**  
Yes. Wegovy and Zepbound are FDA-approved specifically for weight loss in patients without diabetes. You need a qualifying BMI.

**Do I need a referral?**  
No. Telehealth platforms allow direct-to-patient prescribing without a referral.

**Will my insurance cover it?**  
Insurance coverage for weight loss medications varies widely. Many plans do not cover GLP-1s for weight loss, though coverage is improving. We recommend checking with your insurance directly.

**What if I stop seeing results?**  
Your provider can adjust your dose, switch medications, or address other factors. Don't discontinue on your own — work with your care team.
